It was 2:20 am, and the loud wedding music woke me up. Someone got married, and the party was right outside my house. I'm 24 now, and today I saw on Instagram that a friend from my junior college is engaged. That got me thinking, why are people in such a hurry to get married? Don't they have their own dreams and plans? Especially when it's not even a love marriage.

Then I started worrying about next year when I'll be 25. Some of my friends might start settling down with their high school or college sweethearts. It made me sad to think about going to their weddings alone because I haven't really kept in touch with many people after college. We all went our separate ways for different reasons.

The music finally stopped at 2:45 am, and I remembered I had an exam the next day. Yeah, I haven't graduated yet. It took me longer than my friends, not because I failed but because I made some bad choices. But you know what? Things turned out okay for me in my career, and I'm happy with where I am. Still, the feeling of being left behind sometimes bothers me.

Now, in my final year of college, I have mixed feelings about the whole thing. I took a break for two years before joining college, just wanting to enjoy the experience without any big goals like getting a degree or a job. Every day, I think about dropping out, but somehow, I convince myself to stick around until the end. It's like how I deal with my exam preparations - finish what I started.
